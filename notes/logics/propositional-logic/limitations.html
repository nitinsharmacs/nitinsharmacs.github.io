    Limitations

    1. It only consists of propositional and formulas. It doesn’t have relationships among objects.
    2. Not able to express complex sentences and relations.

    1. Alice and Bob know drawing

    How can it be represented in PL?

    Something like this?

    \displaylinesAliceKnowsDrawing∧BobKnowsDrawing

    2. All students know drawing

    How can it be represented in propositional logic?

    \displaylinesAIsStudent→AKnowsDrawingBIsStudent→BKnowsDrawingCIsStudent→CKnowsDrawing...
    1. No notion for categories and individuals.
    2. It is missing objects and relations or predicates. For example, AliceKnowsDrawing can be decomposed into Alice, knows, drawing where Alice and drawing are two objects connected with a predicate knows.

    References

    1. Limitations of propositional logic
    2. First-Order Logic: Syntax and Semantics https://web.engr.oregonstate.edu/~afern/GOFAI/notes/first-order-logic-syntax-semantics.pdf
